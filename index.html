Quaid-I-Azam M. Ali Jinnah

Dec25,1876 - Sep11,1948

National Hero Of Pakistan

The father of the country is our greatest hero. Muhammad Ali Jinnah was his name. His efforts resulted in the creation of Pakistan. He was born on December 25, 1876.Muhammad Ali Jinnah completed his lawyer training in London. He was a member of the Indian National Congress from the beginning. He was a fervent supporter of national politics. He desired to see Hindus and Muslims working together. He conducted numerous conversations between the two communities for this reason. As a result, he was named the Hindu Muslim Unity Ambassador. With time, he learned that the Hindus desired to rule themselves.
As a result, he resigned from the Indian National Congress. Following that, he began attempting to establish a separateMuslim community. He became a member of the All India Muslim League.His contributions aided in the formation of the Pakistani concept. The famous Pakistan Resolution was passed on March 23 . After the British authorities left, it laid the groundwork for our country. The first Governor-General was Quaid-e-Azam. On September 11, 1948, he died suddenly and was laid to rest in Karachi. We should, like him, continue to work to improve our country. As a result of his contributions, he is regarded as one of Pakistan's greatest heroes.

Biographies

  • Born on December 25, 1876, in a prominent mercantile family in Karachi
  • Jinnah joined the Lincoln's Inn in 1893 to become the youngest Indian to be called to the Bar
  • Jinnah rose to prominence and became Bombay's most successful lawyer
  • Jinnah formally entered politics in 1905 from the platform of the Indian National Congress
  • at the Calcutta Congress session (December 1906) first political speech in Calcutta
  • Become a great leader in few years
  • Mr Jinnah died in 1948
